## Should You Sell Your Instagram Handle?

Before thinking about how to sell, think about whether you should. Some considerations:

**Good reasons to sell:**
- You have a handle you no longer use that has genuine market value
- A brand or creator has approached you with a real offer
- You want to monetise an asset that would otherwise sit dormant

**Reasons to be cautious:**
- If the account has followers and content you've built, selling the handle means losing the account entirely
- If the handle is tied to your real name or existing brand, selling it could create confusion
- If you're uncertain about the buyer's intentions, the account could be repurposed in ways you find uncomfortable

If you decide to proceed, here's how to do it right.

## What Is Your Handle Worth?

The single most common mistake sellers make is either wildly overpricing or underpricing. See our [handle valuation guide](/blog/how-to-value-an-instagram-handle) for the full framework. Quick summary:

- **4-character handles:** ₹40,000–₹3,00,000+
- **5-character handles:** ₹12,000–₹1,00,000
- **6-character handles:** ₹8,000–₹50,000
- **7–10 character handles:** ₹5,000–₹30,000
- **Keyword-rich or niche handles:** add 20–50% premium
- **Clean violation history + account age:** significant positive factor
- **Authentic followers:** material premium for 10k+ real followers

Research recent sales in similar categories before setting your price. A broker can give you a market-rate assessment.

## Preparing Your Account for Sale

Before listing, clean up your account:

1. **Remove any content that violates community guidelines** — a listing with a clean account history sells faster and at a higher price
2. **Unlink any third-party apps** that have account access — go to Settings → Apps and Websites and revoke all access
3. **Confirm you have sole access** to the email address and phone number registered to the account — you will need to remove these during the transfer
4. **Do not change the username** while in active negotiations — this removes the product you've agreed to sell
5. **Document the current state** — screenshot your account stats, violation status, and settings before listing

## The Listing Process at IDsvault

Sellers list through Telegram using the /sell command or by contacting the broker directly. You'll provide:
- The handle
- Your asking price
- Current follower count
- Niche/category
- A brief description

The broker verifies ownership (typically by asking you to temporarily change your bio to a specific verification phrase), confirms account status, and lists the handle within 24 hours of verification.

**There is no listing fee.** The broker's commission is a percentage of the final sale price, taken from the buyer's payment — you receive the net amount after commission.

## The Transfer Process

When a buyer commits and payment is confirmed with the broker, the transfer is scheduled. You'll need:
- 30–60 minutes of availability for the live call
- A stable internet connection
- Both parties on a video call with screen sharing

On the call:
1. You share your screen showing your Instagram account settings
2. You change your username to a new handle of your choice (the broker will help you choose one that's available)
3. The buyer immediately claims the released handle on their device
4. Buyer confirms on screen that the handle is in their account
5. Broker confirms transfer and releases your payment

**Payment arrives within a few hours** of the confirmed transfer, via UPI/NEFT to your registered bank account.

## What Sellers Should Never Do

- **Never agree to transfer before payment is held by the broker.** If a buyer asks you to transfer the handle first and "pay right after," this is a classic theft pattern. No legitimate buyer will object to a broker-held payment structure.

- **Never share your account login credentials.** The transfer does not require the buyer to log into your account. If anyone asks for your password or verification code, end the conversation.

- **Never accept crypto, gift cards, or informal IOUs.** INR bank transfer only, through the broker.

- **Don't list on multiple platforms simultaneously.** Accepting a reservation on IDsvault and then selling to someone else directly will result in a permanent ban from the platform and potential legal exposure.

## After the Sale

Once the transfer is complete and payment received, your obligations are done. The broker provides a written summary for both parties. You have no further responsibility for how the buyer uses the handle — though obviously the account should have been handed over in the agreed condition.
