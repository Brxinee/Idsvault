## Instagram's Official Username Requirements

Before buying a handle, it helps to understand what Instagram actually allows — because violations of username rules can cause problems during and after a transfer.

**Official requirements as of 2025:**
- 1 to 30 characters
- Only letters (A–Z, a–z), numbers (0–9), underscores (`_`), and periods (`.`)
- No spaces
- Cannot end with a period
- Cannot contain consecutive periods (`..`)
- Username must be unique across all Instagram accounts

That's the full technical ruleset. Notice what's not there: no minimum length, no requirement for letters, no restriction on starting with a number.

## Reserved and Restricted Handles

Instagram maintains a list of reserved usernames that cannot be registered by regular users. These include:
- Internal function names and paths (`/accounts`, `/explore`, `/direct`, etc.)
- Trademarked brand names where Instagram has received a formal trademark claim
- Handles previously held by verified public figures who have passed away (these are memorialised)
- A rotating list of high-profile handles that Instagram holds in reserve for future institutional use

If you're trying to acquire a handle that appears "available" in search but can never be registered, it may be in this reserved category.

## The ToS Prohibition on Transfers

Instagram's Terms of Service state that accounts cannot be sold or transferred. Specifically, they prohibit selling, licensing, or otherwise transferring your account or any rights to your account.

This creates a legal grey area for the secondary market. The prohibition exists in the ToS but Instagram has no formal enforcement mechanism for detecting transfers — they enforce through reports, behaviour patterns, and algorithm flags rather than monitoring username changes.

**Practical implications:**
- Transfers that look like organic username changes carry low enforcement risk
- Transfers that are publicly announced ("just bought this handle, DM me for deals") carry high risk
- Accounts with prior violations are more likely to be reviewed after a transfer
- Multiple rapid username changes on the same account can trigger a temporary lock

## What "Account Age" Means in Practice

Instagram's internal trust systems assign higher trust to older accounts. This affects everything from comment reach to story visibility to the likelihood of surviving a ToS review. When you buy a handle on an aged account (2012–2016 registration), you inherit that trust score — a significant advantage for brands starting from zero.

Conversely, a new account (created in the last 6 months) with a great handle but no age offers little trust benefit. The handle's value in this case is almost purely the name itself.

## Username Changes and the Availability Window

When an account changes its username, the old username typically becomes available within a few minutes to a few hours. There are exceptions:
- Usernames from deactivated accounts may be held for up to 14 days
- Usernames from accounts that were suspended may never become available
- Instagram occasionally holds certain usernames for internal review before releasing them

For broker-supervised transfers, the window timing is managed on the live call. Both parties coordinate the release and claim simultaneously to minimise the window where a third party could intervene.

## What to Check Before Buying Any Handle

1. **Verify the current username exactly** — no hidden characters, look-alikes, or Unicode substitutions
2. **Check account status** — is it currently active, restricted, or in any limited mode?
3. **Review follower/following ratio** for signs of artificial inflation
4. **Confirm account age** via the earliest post or creation date services
5. **Ask for a screenshot of account settings** showing no pending violations or appeals

A broker who completes these checks before listing is providing verification you would otherwise have to do yourself — and doing it with more access than a buyer typically has before committing.
